<p><p>The long-term hemodialysis catheter market is primarily divided into silicone and polyurethane types. Silicone catheters are known for their biocompatibility, flexibility, and resistance to thrombosis, making them suitable for extended use. On the other hand, polyurethane catheters are characterized by their durability and strength, offering a lower risk of infection due to their smooth surfaces. Both materials cater to the needs of patients requiring long-term dialysis, with their specific properties influencing choice based on patient conditions and clinical requirements.</p></p>
