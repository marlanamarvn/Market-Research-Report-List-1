<p><p>The magnetic stripe card market is divided into two primary types based on coercivity: high coercivity and low coercivity cards. High coercivity cards can store more data and offer enhanced security, making them suitable for applications like credit cards and secure identification. Low coercivity cards are easier to swipe but less secure, often used for simpler applications like loyalty cards and basic access control. The choice between them depends on the security needs and data storage requirements of the user.</p></p>
