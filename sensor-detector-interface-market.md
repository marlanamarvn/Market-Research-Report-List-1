<p><p>The Sensor & Detector Interface market is categorized into two primary types: analog and digital. Analog interfaces provide continuous signal output, representing sensor data in varying voltage or current levels. They are often used in applications requiring straightforward, real-time environmental feedback. Conversely, digital interfaces convert sensor data into discrete signals, enabling easier integration with microcontrollers and digital systems. Digital systems offer advantages such as noise immunity and enhanced data processing capabilities, making them suitable for modern automation and IoT applications.</p></p>
