<p><p>The compound semiconductor materials market encompasses various types, including III-V, II-VI, IV-IV, and sapphire materials. III-V semiconductors, such as gallium arsenide, are known for high electron mobility and are widely used in optoelectronics. II-VI materials, like cadmium selenide, are pivotal for infrared optics and solar cells. IV-IV materials, primarily silicon-germanium alloys, have applications in high-speed electronics. Lastly, sapphire, a crystalline substrate, is essential for LEDs and other optoelectronic components due to its excellent optical properties and thermal stability.</p></p>
