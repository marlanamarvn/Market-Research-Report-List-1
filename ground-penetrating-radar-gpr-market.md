<p><p>Ground Penetrating Radar (GPR) is a non-invasive geophysical method that utilizes radar pulses to image the subsurface. It is employed across various sectors including construction, archaeology, environmental studies, and utility mapping to detect and delineate buried structures, utilities, and geological features. GPR systems consist of a transmitter that sends radar waves into the ground and a receiver that captures reflected signals, providing detailed images of subsurface conditions.</p><p>The Ground Penetrating Radar (GPR) Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increased infrastructure development and urbanization. Expanding applications in different industries, including civil engineering, mining, and oil & gas, are further propelling market demand. Technological advancements, such as portable and user-friendly GPR systems, are enhancing usability and access.
