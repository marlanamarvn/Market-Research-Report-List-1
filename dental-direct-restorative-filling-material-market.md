<p><p>The dental direct restorative filling material market comprises several types, including composite fillings, amalgam fillings, glass ionomer fillings, and others. Composite fillings are resin-based materials that mimic natural tooth color, ideal for aesthetics and versatility. Amalgam fillings consist of a durable mixture of metals and are commonly used for posterior teeth due to their strength. Glass ionomer fillings release fluoride and bond chemically to the tooth structure, making them suitable for specific cases. Other materials may include resin ionomers and compomers, catering to diverse dental needs.</p></p>
