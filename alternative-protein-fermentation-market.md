<p><p>The alternative protein fermentation market encompasses three primary types. Traditional fermentation uses microorganisms to convert organic substrates into protein-rich products, commonly seen in foods like yogurt and cheese. Biomass fermentation harnesses the rapid growth of microbial biomass, producing high-protein ingredients from fungal or bacterial sources. Precision fermentation leverages advanced biotechnological methods to cultivate specific proteins directly from microbes, enabling the production of animal-like proteins without livestock. Together, these methods offer sustainable solutions for protein needs in food and feed industries.</p></p>
